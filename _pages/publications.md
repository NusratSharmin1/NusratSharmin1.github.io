## Working Papers

**"Climate Shocks and Gender Gaps in Early-Life Survival in Bangladesh"** (2026)

This paper examines how cyclone exposure affects infant mortality and child health investment in Bangladesh, using DHS survey data matched to IBTrACS cyclone tracks in a difference-in-differences framework with district and month-year fixed effects.

## Work in Progress

**"When Enforcement Matters: Maternity Leave, Female Employment, and the Gender Gap in Bangladesh"** (2025)

This paper studies the effects of Bangladesh's 2011 maternity leave reform on female employment and gender gaps in the labor market, using a quasi-experimental design.

**Economics of Fire-Based Land Treatments**

Wildfire is one of the most economically consequential natural hazards in the United States. I am part of a research team working on multiple papers investigating whether and how prescribed fire can serve as an effective tool for reducing these economic costs at the community level.
